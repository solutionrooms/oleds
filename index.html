Panel Type Guide

QD-OLED
Samsung S95F, S95H, S85F; Sony Bravia 8 II. Quantum Dot + OLED. Best colour saturation & peak brightness. Excellent for dark rooms and bright content.
Primary RGB Tandem OLED evo
LG G5, G6; Panasonic Z95B. LG's premium panel. Brightest OLED overall. Best for bright rooms and HDR highlights.
Tandem OLED evo
LG C5. Good brightness, excellent contrast. Better than standard WOLED. The C5 outperforms the new C6 55" despite being older.
Standard W-OLED
LG C6 (55"), Samsung S90F/S90H. Good contrast, lower brightness. Entry-level OLED performance. The C6 55" is a step down from the C5 despite being a newer model.
OLED SE (budget)
Panasonic Z86C (2026). LG's new budget OLED panel — removes polariser for lower cost. Lowest brightness tier. Good entry point for OLED.

⚠️ CORRECTION: LG C6 55" Uses Standard WOLED — Not Tandem

The LG C6H designation applies only to the 77" and 83" models, which use the premium Primary RGB Tandem OLED panel. The 55" model is the LG C6 (not C6H) and uses a standard W-OLED panel — the same type as the LG C4 (2024). This is a meaningful downgrade versus the C5 55", which used Tandem OLED evo. At £1,496 it's priced similarly to the C5 was at this point — but offers inferior panel technology.

Recommendation: If you want a 2026 LG OLED with a premium panel, go for the G6 55" (~£1,499 mid-May) which uses Primary RGB Tandem OLED evo. Or buy the LG C5 now at £999 effective — better panel, lower price.
