Samsung S95H Now On Sale in US — UK Launch Imminent

The S95H launched for sale in the US on 2 April 2026, with confirmed pricing:

  • 55" — $2,499
  • 65" — $3,400
  • 77" — $4,500
  • 83" — $6,500

UK pricing has not been officially announced, but with the US launch now live, a UK release is expected within weeks. Based on the S95F's cadence (US April → UK late April/May), an announcement could come any day. Samsung's 2026 OLED lineup has been described as launching "globally" from April 2026.

Key upgrades over S95F: 35% brighter (up to 3,000 nits peak), new FloatLayer Design with metallic bezel, OLED HDR Pro with Glare Free 3.0, optional HDMI expansion box, Art Mode (first time on a Samsung OLED).

Note: 48" and 83" S95H use LG Tandem W-OLED; 55", 65", 77" retain Samsung QD-OLED.

Sources: SamMobile (US launch) | Gizmochina (global) | What Hi-Fi? hands-on | DisplaySpecifications

Samsung S95F 55" Hits Lowest-Ever UK Price

With Samsung now selling its 2026 flagship in the US, the S95F 55" has dropped to its lowest-ever UK price at £1,499, down from a £2,499 launch price — a drop of £1,000 in under a year. Both What Hi-Fi? and TechRadar have flagged this as a record low for the 2025 TV of the Year.

LG G6 and C6H Are Here — Cheaper Than Last Year

LG has confirmed UK pricing for its 2026 OLED range. The C6H 55" is £1,496 — competitive with where the C5 ended up after months of discounting. The G6 is available for pre-order; 48" starts at £1,699 and 65" at £2,999 (55" price not yet confirmed, estimated ~£2,199). Both feature upgraded Tandem OLED panels with improved brightness over 2025 equivalents.
